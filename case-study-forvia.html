Context

  • Built in a collaboration between CentraleSupélec L2S Lab and Forvia.
  • Project explored whether lower-cost sensing could support robust localization in controlled conditions.

Problem

  • Single-sensor signals were not sufficiently robust for stable localization on their own.
  • The team needed a fusion prototype that could combine a camera prior with array-based measurements.

Constraints

  • Sensor alignment and synchronization had to remain stable across test conditions.
  • Camera priors and array likelihoods had different noise and resolution characteristics.
  • Prototype scope focused on controlled test-area validation rather than full production deployment.

Approach

  • Built a camera-derived spatial prior from image-processing outputs.
  • Generated spatial likelihood maps from array measurements and fused them with an optimal-transport-based objective.
  • Validated localization behavior through controlled experiments and shared findings with partners.

Results

  • Produced a working multi-sensor localization pipeline for controlled experiments.
  • Authored a research report documenting the setup, method, and findings.
